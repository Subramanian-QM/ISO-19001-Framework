What is ISO 19011?

📌
Purpose & Scope
ISO 19011 provides guidance on auditing management systems, including the principles of auditing, managing an audit programme and conducting management system audits. It is applicable to all organisations needing to conduct internal or external audits.
🌐
Universal Application
Applicable to any organisation regardless of size or type. The standard covers quality, environmental, health & safety, information security, and all other management system disciplines under a single unified framework.
📅
2018 Revision Key Changes
Introduced a risk-based approach to auditing, guidance on remote auditing techniques, expanded competency requirements, and enhanced guidance on managing audit programme risks and opportunities.
🔄
Relationship to Other Standards
Supports audit programmes for ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001, ISO 27001 and all other management system standards. Can be used alongside sector-specific audit guidelines for combined and joint audits.

ISO 19011 recognises multiple audit types based on the relationship between auditor and auditee, and the purpose of the audit engagement.

Audit Programme Management

An audit programme is the set of one or more audits planned for a specific time frame and directed towards a specific purpose. Effective management follows a PDCA cycle.
